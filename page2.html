Video Games started growing more intensely when the development of arcades started. Since being in many public spaces, it helped further bring the genre out into the open. From there, video games continued to evolve with technology, creativity, and community support—turning a niche hobby into a global phenomenon enjoyed by millions of people of all ages.



The rise of arcades in the late 1970s and early 1980s introduced exciting, interactive experiences that people could enjoy outside their homes. Iconic games like Pac-Man, Space Invaders, and Donkey Kong became cultural sensations, attracting players with simple yet addictive gameplay. As technology advanced, home consoles brought gaming into living rooms, allowing friends and families to play together. The internet then connected players around the world, creating vibrant online communities and competitive eSports scenes. Today, video games offer immersive stories, stunning graphics, and endless genres—from action and adventure to strategy and simulation—making them a powerful form of entertainment, creativity, and connection.
